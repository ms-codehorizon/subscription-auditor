# Response Templates

## A. Standard cancellation steps (in-app services)

> Cancel [SERVICE]: [Account/Settings path if commonly known, else
> "Settings → Subscription → Cancel"]. Effective [end of billing cycle].
> Before cancelling: [export/data steps, or "nothing to export"].
> Saves $[X]/mo ($[X*12]/yr).

## B. Retention-offer ask (use before cancelling when rule 6a fires)

> Subject: Cancellation - [SERVICE]
>
> Hi - I've been a subscriber since [year]. The price has risen from
> $[old] to $[new] and I'm planning to cancel at the end of this cycle.
> If you have a retention offer or a legacy rate available, I'd
> consider staying. I would also accept pausing my subscription for
> a few months if that's an option. Otherwise please confirm
> cancellation effective [date].

## C. Work reimbursement request (rule 5b)

> Subject: Expense approval - [SERVICE] ($[X]/mo)
>
> Hi [manager] - I use [SERVICE] primarily for [work purpose, one
> line]. Could this move to a company expense / team license? Happy to
> provide usage details.

## D. Audit report format (every audit MUST follow this exactly)

Style rules: plain hyphens only, NEVER em-dashes. No preamble or
closing remarks. Keep every table cell under 12 words.

**1. Summary block (first, always):**

> **$X/mo total spend · $Y/mo potential savings ($Z/yr) · N cancel,
> N downgrade, N flag, N keep**

**2. The table** - one terse line per item, ordered: Section 2 urgent
renewals first, then CANCELs, DOWNGRADEs, FLAGs, KEEPs:

| Subscription | $/mo | Decision | Rule | Why (max 12 words) |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**3. Execute this week** - numbered list, one line per action, only
items needing action in the next 7 days.

**4. Drafts appendix** - ONLY items that need a written message
(retention asks per 6a, reimbursement per 5b). One heading per item,
then the draft. Cancellation steps for ordinary cancels stay in the
table's Why column or Execute list - do not write essays for them.
